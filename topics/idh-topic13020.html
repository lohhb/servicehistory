Service History

Subject: STM NS-8080 Low vacuum reported leading to device pick up error from index arm

Handler Model: NS-8080 (S/N: 181581)

Controller: RC520

Date: 22 Jul 2010


 

Symptom

STM NS-8080 Low vacuum reported leading to device pick up error from index arm.


 


 

Action

1)Found problem to be not low vacuum but rather due to no contact air, contactor head did not sit into pocket for the pick up. Intermittently depending if the down motion, contactor was able to sit into pocket for pick up.


 

2) Found EP(Electronic Pneumatic) regulator reading is 0 for 3kgf. #1 showed 3kpa at least.

Checked SETUP and found compliant cylinder set at 55mm although it should be 40mm for 2x2 quad. After changed to 40mm, EP reading showed 7kpa. So far no device pick up error.


 

3) Because of EP no reading, contact test unable to pass after contact height calibration. With 7kpa, site A&B able to pass after contact height calibration. Only site C&D open contact.

4) Increased contact height to pass C&D and contact force from 0.245N to 0.35N but deep probe mark on ball. N.G.

5) To check and verify on docking the next day. Temporary disable site D for overnight production run and monitor for jam.

6) Suspect socket mount N.G, docking N.G as C&D open contact as VM check showed probe mark was not center of ball. The markings showed almost out of ball suggesting alignment issue of device and socket. Note socket design has floating plate assembly which could mean potential issue to device stuck and device alignment with socket.

7) SLK with contactor head without index arm used to try for contact test.

But C&D unable to pass contact suspecting possible tilt below socket guide plate onwards.


 

8)Meeting with Meilin for double device sensor for different socket size/docking height as STM has many thickness of socket.

9) KA to come up with design for "variable" socket sensor and EPSON to confirm on solution as Meilin wants socket sensor to have ability to adjust based on socket thickness/docking height.


 


 

Cause

Low vacuum reported leading to device pick up error from index arm, found problem to be not low vacuum but rather due to no contact air (because wrong compliant cylinder setting), contactor head did not sit into pocket for the pick up.
