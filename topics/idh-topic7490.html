Service History

Subject: Z-axis motor in index arm 1 cannot release brake

Handler Model: NS-6040(CARSEM, S/N:12903, SEIKO04)

Controller: SRC520

Date: 14 Aug 2008


 

Symptom

Z-axis motor in index arm 1 cannot release brake.


 


 

Action

After changing 'Z' axis motor of index arm 1, we did an initial teaching on index robot and shuttles.  While doing teaching, we found out that 'U' axis motor of index arm 2 also had the same problem, that is, cannot release brake.

Took 'U' axis motor from SEIKO03 and fitted into this handler.

After fitting this good motor, teaching carried out in index and shuttle area found good.

Teaching was also carried out on all other robots.  After teaching, we did a homing with SRC error on index arm and we found out that a plate on the index arm 1 is preventing index arm 2 from homing, thus plate removed and homing was good.

Upon running dry run, SRC error came on again indicating that the pose data for empty tray arm are not defined.  After much troubleshooting found out that this handler in CARSEM was modified from drawer to colour tray, thus, an additional point of P43 was required to be taught.

After teaching P43, no more SRC error.

During Dry run, we noticed that input arm hand A got problem in picking from pocket (hand A only lowered 2-5mm and then returned to top position).  After much troubleshooting, we suspect that the stepper motor is not functioning correctly.  Currently, we removed the spring on hand A, and it is able to pick from pocket.

After all this, we try to run some dummy and notice that index arm1 hand B do not have vacuum, suspect that vacuum unit is not working.  We by-pass site 2 and did a run on the dummy and was successful.  Fine tuning was also carried out while running the dummy.

Current status is to try out a new stepper motor or spring on input arm hand A and to replace the vacuum unit for index arm 1 hand B. It could also be stepper driver problem.


 


 

Cause

- faulty Z-axis motor in index arm 1 cause built-in break cannot release brake.

- faulty U-axis motor in index arm 1 cause built-in break cannot release brake.

- SRC error came on again indicating that the pose data for empty tray arm are not defined.

After much troubleshooting found out that this handler in CARSEM was modified from Manual Drawer to colour tray, thus, an additional point of P43 was required to be taught.
