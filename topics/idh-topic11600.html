Subject: NS-7000 MMI H7.11-SSY issues

Handler Model: NS-7000

Controller: RC520

Date: 19 Jan 2010


 

Symptom

SCS faced some issue on NS7000. As for the software version is MMI H7.11-SSY.

Qin Li, Keok Boon and I had tired out on the handler in 2 situations.

 

Situation #1

After Arm 1 pickup devices from shuttle, we pause the handler and turn off the Site D vacuum sensitivity manually. Then we depressed "Start" button and "Device drop Error (Arm1)" occurred. "Skip Enable" , "Skip" and Alarm Reset" came on. We turn on the vacuum sensitivity manually, followed by depressing "Skip Enable", ""Skip" and "Start" button. Arm 1 plunged into the socket and a start of test signal was send to the tester. Bin 1 was asisgned to the arm 1 Site D by the tester but after test, Site D was asigned to Error Bin. 

 

Situation #2

After Arm 1 pickup devices from shuttle, we pause the handler and removed device from Site D manually. Then we depressed "Start" button and "Device drop Error (Arm1)" occurred. "Skip Enable" , "Skip" and Alarm Reset" came on. We the device back on to Arm 1 Site D manually, followed by depressing "Skip Enable", ""Skip" and "Start" button. Arm 1 plunged into the socket and a start of test signal was send to the tester. Bin 1 was assigned to the arm 1 Site D by the tester but after test, Site D device was sorted out to Error Bin. 

 

Enquiries

 

1) Why does this happen? Normal?

2) Why does handler send a SOT signal for Arm 1 Site D to the Tester?

3) Why Site D device was sorted out to error bin instead of Bin 1 even though the tester assigned a Bin 1 to Site D device?


 

This is serious causing test result integrity issue.

Is there any other situation that after error skip and test device will be bin out accordingly instead of “Error” bin?


 

20 Jan 2010 email reply from Goto

In NS7000/NS8000 series, when device drop error is caused in between Input Shuttle and Socket, the device, which is putting back to socket manually, goes to Error Bin. It is software design for NS7000/NS8000 series and different from the sequence of NS6000


 

The background of this change is concerning the possibility that device may have damage after it is dropped even if test result is OK.


 

Email by SCS

There’re actually two issues here.

1. When we did the simulation, the unit was tested but sorted to error bin. This caused test summary not tally to physical bin. As per your explanation, the software is written to sort the unit to error bin. In this case, handler should not send SOT to test this unit.


 

2. During production, when there’s intermittent sensor error at arm 2, handler gives device drop error on arm1. After skip, all units are tested and sorted according to the test result. No unit sorted to error bin. This must be software bug need to be fixed.
