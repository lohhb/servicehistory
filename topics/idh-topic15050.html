Subject: NS-7000 Output Hand cannot close to 57.15mm at Shuttle 2

Handler Model: NS-7000

Controller: RC520

Date: 29 Jul 2011


 

Symptom

ASE NS7000 training handler, output arm pitch at shuttle 1 able to close to x-pitch of 57.15mm but at shuttle 2, about 59mm and onwards, unable to close to 57.15mm.

Feedback from trainees another handler is having this problem but on the input arm.

80x60 pitch is ok and other handlers, able to run this package with no problems.


 


 

Action

During shuttle 1 pickup, Output Hand will close to 57.15mm from 80mm after initialization.

After 1st 4 devices pickup and go to unloader on normal z-mode release, goes to shuttle 2 on 59mm and onwards, unable to close to 57.15mm.

Found during close pitch, unable to close fully due to open/close sensor flap hit to fixture. So after the first close pitch, unable to open to correct x-pitch of 57.15mm.

Loosen and ensure sensor flap does not hit anything upon close pitch.

Dry run verified and ok.

Checked on another reported handler on the input arm with this issue, found same problem. Sensor flap hit fixture and unable to close fully.


 


 


 

Cause

Output Hand unable to close fully due to open/close sensor hit to fixture.


 


 

Remarks

1)   ASE NS7000 pitching out at 83mm instead of 80mm.

2)   Output arm hand A and B out on initial start.

3)   Close pitch looks ok as compared to input arm.

4)   Variable pitch offset is 0.

5)   Mechanical as reported by customer should be ok as A and B offset by same pitch and does not look slant.

6)   Pose data manipulator 7 and 8 does not contain open/close point data due to early handler. Office NS7000 also does not have open/close point data.

7)   Another handler of later serial number, able to see pose data of 60mm.

8)   As point data not available, have to execute command.

9)   Advice to check 80mm pitch for open and 20mm for close pitch by command.

10) mcal to be 80mm pitch and to execute Go P* + x(60) for 20mm close pitch.

11) If mcal is not 80mm, it may mean HOFS is out or home sensor to be adjusted.

12)             During mcal and command, to see if A and B hit against limit in 80/20 pitch.
