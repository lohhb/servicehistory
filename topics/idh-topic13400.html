Service History

Subject: EPSON finding on Xilinx Octal Layout kit(device drop & Index arm stuck in socket)

Handler Model: NS-7080W

Controller: RC520

Date: 21 Sep 2010


 

Symptom

EPSON's findings were based on XAP SLK sent to them as follows:


 

1) Type of O-ring is reason for vacuum leak. Verification with following contact force @ 125kPa, 250kPa, 450kPa.

Only orange and white type is confirmed by EPSON, no vacuum leak even till 450kPa.

Blue type, has vacuum leak at 250kPa and 450kPa.


 

Therefore, EPSON's conclusion is blue type O-ring, is not appropriate for handler's application as it causes vacuum leak.


 

2) EPSON did not experience the "gap" with the SLK sent to them so they are unable to verify the cause of "gap" and the effect of shims.

But it means the hook condition of the SLK is still OK condition as they did'nt see the gap. The difference here is the possible causes as follows:

a) Latch condition is N.G. The spring is for purpose of pulling(holding) the SLK upwards when it locks. If spring loses it's tension, only possible way is to adds shims to increase the mounting height.

b) If wrong O-ring is used, @ 100+kPa CDA coupled with bad condition of latch, it may cause the "dancing effect"

c) Very bad condition of guide pins/guide pin hole. From EPSON's point of view, 2 guide pins + 1 orientation guide, once SLK latched on, very unlikely for "dancing effect".

d) NS7KW-03 was sent back to XAP from SPIL and the handler condition is quite bad as you may know. There may have been pervious crashes or bad condition. The "dancing" effect is really classic and we believe it is a special and could be a one time case due to combination of factors mentioned above.

e) From Loh's finding of wrong diameter guide pin used, it possibly means the constant "tight fit" and rubbing may have cause deterioration slowly to latch condition as it didn't cause the SLK stuck immediately upon first insertion. In this case, it may indicate the latches had been under stress leading to bad condition. Or in the case of the SLK hook angle change for example.


 

10kg spec is the hardware spec of 1 TAKIGEN latch(wrong latch) so based on the weight of SLK sent to EPSON, current design of 2 latches is sufficient to hold it and the "gap" or "dancing" should not happen. If due to guide pin dimensions out should SLK be stuck, definately this will be more than 20kg.


 

To summarize, as EPSON did not encounter the "gap" on XAP SLK, they are unable to comment if shims is a must. Their opinion is as long as if it helps in XAP point of view/experience, it can be used. The current design of 2 latches with a combine capability of 20kg with 2 guide pins and orientation pin, it is sufficient. EPSON does not mean the O-ring is the cause of the gap.


 

Remarks

For Index Arm stuck in socket, check:

1) Guide pin dimension(eg diameter & tolerance) on socket guide plate

2) Metal latch on Index test Hand is correct specification, use correct T fastern(Latch) of part No is TL-29A ( R335N700011700 ), supplied by Tochigiya.

3) Teach point P50 & 51 is correct

4) Index Arm alignment


 

For Device drop at Index, check:

1) O-ring on octal layout kit surface is of correct type( orange or the translucent white)

2) Air fitting is secured on octal layout kit

3) Peek material washer(not white colour) should be used on air fitting for octal layout kit(0199-NS7K)

4) Air tubing on layout kit is not broken

5) O-ring in plunger of contact is not worn out or broken (0255-NS7K)

6) Plunger in contactor able to retract(clean plunger)

7) Suction cup protrude abit from device pickup surface of nest

8) Vacuum is not weak(check filter is not dirty and filter's cover is secured properly)

9) Vacuum sensitivity is set correctly on Index vacuum switch

10) Vacuum valve is working(manual trigger vacuum or by MMI I/O monitor)

11) Remote I/O card (0210-NS7K)

12) I/O Module


 

Check points for Index Arm Device drop:

- vacuum cup condition, device drop more frequently in hot mode?

- o-ring worn out on plunger

-Check diameter/tolerance of guide pins on socket guide plate

-Check any broken/bent tubing or tubing pressed by cable tie on SLK

-Check air fitting/elbow or gasket not loose on SLK

-Check if guide pins bent/worn out

-Check vacuum filter dirty

-Check vacuum filter put properly

-Check seal for vacuum put properly and not torn

-Check plastic casing for vacuum filter not broken

-Check nest guide pin's hole

-Check o-ring on SLK

-Check metal hooks of Socket Layout Kit (SLK)

-Check correct brand and specification of latch use for (Index) Test Hands

-Check metal latch worn out at Index Test Hand

-Check Index teaching position P50 and P51

-Check if Index Arm crash before or Index Arm bent(Index Arm alignment check)

- if device never drop, adjust vacuum switch sensitivity


 

Correct metal latch of parts No is TL-29A, this is supplied by Tochigiya.
